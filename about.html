The Problem


Many students, workers, parents, and teachers all around the world own many different sizes of cups, from 12 ounces to 42 ounces. Many people either have cup holders that are too large or, too small for their cup holder. The most popular drink in America is coffee, a piping hot beverage with hard to remove stains. If a cup holder is unstable and loose, coffee will get thrown out of the loose cup holder, spilling all the hot coffee over your lap, and eventually creating a car accident. Although a loose cup holder is dangerous, a person's water bottle may not even fit into their car cup holder. This will require them to leave their water bottle on the floor. This will cause their water bottle to fall over, and maybe even break or dent. An additional problem that we are trying to help solve is dehydration. One of the prominent reasons why a lot of people are dehydrated daily is because people are not holding enough water in their bottles. It is also apparent that wherever you go, there is not a guaranteed refilling area. So the only solution to this is to hold bigger bottles, something that is not easily held within the standard cupholders we have today. This is why we need a more adaptable cup holder so that people can hold better bottles that are suited for them. Our main target consumer are people who have larger water bottles or cups. These people need a cup holder that can hold their large bottles but can also hold their smaller bottles without it being so loose. Our team's intention is to keep people themselves safe, and to give all water bottles a comfortable and safe area to be put into.


Our Solution


The problem we are dealing with is how current cup holders found in cars do not fit all sizes of cups and overall are not secure enough the way they are. To solve this problem of cup holders not catering to all users, we decided to make a cup holder that will accomplish this. Our solution is an automated universal cup holder as its main function is to close upon any type of bottle, regardless of size, as well as hold the object tight. The reason why our design is superior to that of prior solutions is because of its flexibility and secureness. Most of the prior solutions, such as the Dorman Cup Holder and the Smart Kup Car Cup Holder, have fixed sizes in which only certain sized bottles are able to fit within them. Even the Delta Cycle Expanding Beverage Holder had limits in terms of how much it could hold. This product was also only usable for bikes. We improve on all of these designs through our product flexibility as well as its overall performance. Our design is simply a display of how our solution will operate. It proposes a system of rails, bump switches and a SONAR sensor in which the SONAR will sense whether an object is present, which will trigger the rails to push the bump switches together. The bump switches, once pressed, will immediately stop and at that point, the bottle will be secure. To release this, a limit switch will be pressed in order to pull back the bump switches and the bottle will then be easily retrieved by the user. For our actual final design, we intend it to be directly installed within cars and utilize malleable metals such as aluminum or steel alloy shines for a sleeker appearance. Future improvements and implications would consist of making it so that the system recognizes tension so that it would release automatically the moment a user grabs the bottle.
